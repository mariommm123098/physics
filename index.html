01. DEFINING SHM & EQUATIONS

SHM Definition: Motion where acceleration is proportional to displacement and directed towards equilibrium.

a = -ω²x

The minus sign indicates the Restoring Force is opposite to displacement.

Kinematics (Start at x=x₀):

  • Displacement: x = x₀ cos(ωt)
  • Velocity: v = -v₀ sin(ωt) (v₀ = ωx₀)
  • V-x Relationship: v = ±ω√(x₀² - x²)

02. ENERGY IN SHM

Energy transforms between Kinetic (Ek) and Potential (Ep), but Total Energy (Et) remains constant (if undamped).

  • Ek (Kinetic): Max at equilibrium (x=0). Graph is an inverted parabola.
  • Ep (Potential): Max at amplitude (x=x₀). Graph is a "U" parabola.
  • Total Energy: Et = ½mω²x₀²

Key Exam Point: Total energy is proportional to Amplitude squared (A²).

03. DAMPING & RESONANCE

Damping: Loss of energy due to resistive forces (friction/air resistance).

  • Light: Oscillates with decreasing amplitude. Period T constant.
  • Critical: Returns to equilibrium in shortest time. No oscillation. (e.g., Car suspension).
  • Over: Returns very slowly. No oscillation.

Resonance: Occurs when Driving Frequency = Natural Frequency.

Effect: Max Amplitude. Phase difference = π/2.

GRAPH TIP: Higher damping = Lower peak & Shifted left
